We use Cookies and similar tracking tech to keep tabs on activity on our Service and store certain info. The tracking tech we roll with includes beacons, tags, and scripts, all used to collect and track info, and to fine-tune and analyze our Service. The tech we use might include:

-   **Cookies or Browser Cookies**: A Cookie is a small file dropped onto your Device. You can tell your browser to straight-up refuse all Cookies or to give you a heads-up when one's being sent. -   **Web Beacons**: Certain sections of our Service and our emails might contain tiny electronic files called web beacons (also known as clear gifs, pixel tags, and single-pixel gifs). These bad boys let the Company, for example, count users who've visited those pages or opened an email. They're also used for other related website stats (like checking out how popular a certain section is and verifying system and server integrity).

Cookies can be "Persistent" or "Session" Cookies. Persistent Cookies chill on your personal computer or mobile device even after you go offline, while Session Cookies vanish the moment you close your web browser.

We use both Session and Persistent Cookies for the following reasons:

-   **Necessary / Essential Cookies**
    -   **Type**: Session Cookies
    -   **Administered by**: Us
    -   **Purpose**: These Cookies are absolutely crucial to give you the services available through the Website and to let you use some of its cool features. -   **Cookies Policy / Notice Acceptance Cookies**
    -   **Type**: Persistent Cookies
    -   **Administered by**: Us
    -   **Purpose**: These Cookies are all about figuring out if users have accepted the use of cookies on the Website.

-   **Functionality Cookies**
    -   **Type**: Persistent Cookies
